S12 The Missions

12. THE MISSIONS

 

12.1 INTRODUCTION: Each SASL Mission is divided into ten sections, as follows: Briefing, ENEMY Attitude, Mission Tables, Mapboard Selection & Activation, VP Locations, S? Placement/Entry, Sequence, Mission End, VP Schedule, and Mission Special Rules (MSR).

12.2 MISSION SELECTION: After selecting the desired FRIENDLY nationality (review 18.5 and subsections for applicable nationality distinctions) but prior to Mission setup the player must first determine the Mission's date.  To select a Mission roll on either the "FRIENDLY on Offense" or "FRIENDLY on Defense" column of Table {A7} or select any desired Mission [EXC: see 17.21 -.22 if playing a CG Mission].6

12.21 PARTISAN ENEMY: Each Mission that is marked with a "(P)" following the Mission's ID number (e.g., Mission 2) may have a Partisan ENEMY if the FRIENDLY side meets one of the following conditions:

   •   if German, Italian, or Axis Minor and the Mission takes place during 1942-44 in Russia, the Balkans, or France.

   •   if Japanese and the Mission takes place during 1937-45 in China, Indo-China, or 1942-45 in the Philippines.

   •   if (Vichy) French and the Mission takes place in Indo-China.

Make a pre-Mission dr to determine the ENEMY nationality; a Final dr of ≤ 1 results in a Partisan ENEMY.

Cumulative drm:

   -1 if in Russia in 1943

   -1 if in China, Indo-China, Greece or the Balkans

   -1 if previous Mission was vs. Partisan

12.211  PARTISAN ENEMIES: Partisans use the Partisan Generation Tables and the Partisan-ENEMY RE Table (instead of the {A11}ENEMY RE Table) with the columns for the appropriate areas.

12.212  PARTISAN ENEMY ATTRIBUTES: An Activated Partisan Infantry unit pays 1 MF to enter a woods Location. Captured weapon penalties do not apply to Partisans using Allied SW/Guns generated from a Partisan generation Table (only). Partisans have MOL capability (A22.6) which should be employed whenever beneficial. Partisans may not Deploy, may not participate in multi-Location FG, and may not make Entrenching Attempts. A Partisan Commissar affects another Partisan unit just as if both were 10/42 Russians [EXC: if he fails to rally a squad it is Replaced by its two HS].

12.213  PARTISAN ENEMY ACTIVATION TABLE: Reroll each table A1 result containing AFV or SPG unless the Mission takes place in the Balkans, in which case the {P6} table is used.

12.22 SPECIAL FORCES INTEGRITY:  If the first ENEMY MMC generated {x2} from a non RE S? is an MMC type which (under historical circumstances) would not normally be intermixed with other, different type/class MMC of that nationality (e.g., US paratrooper [7-4-7], German SS [6-5-8] or British Commando [6-4-8]), all subsequent non-RE S? that successfully Activate a MMC can only generate that same MMC type (no roll on Table {x2} is required). Similarly, if the first ENEMY MMC generated {x2} from a RE S? is an MMC type which (as per above) would not normally be intermixed with other, different type/class MMC of that nationality, all subsequent S? from that same RE that successfully Activate a MMC can only generate that same MMC type (no roll on Table {x2} is required).

EX: If the ENEMY nationality is German and the first MMC generated {G2} from a non-RE S? is an "SS" MMC, all subsequent non-RE S? that successfully Activate a MMC can only generate "SS" MMC {G2}. Additionally, if the first MMC generated {G2} from a German ENEMY RE S? is an "SS" MMC, all subsequent S? that successfully Activate occurring due to the same RE can only generate "SS" MMC {G2}.

12.23 THEATER OF OPERATIONS: The player choses the appropriate Mission theater of operations based on the following*:

   •      Western European Theater: Norway and Europe west of central Germany, including Italy, Greece, and the Mediterranean Islands. Can include normal terrain, bocage (B9.5), and vineyard (F13.6).

   •      North Africa Theater: North Africa, Ethiopia, Dakar and the Middle East. Can include Desert (F.), alternate (F13.), and normal terrain.

   •      Eastern European Theater: Finland, Europe east of central Germany, the Balkans (excluding Greece), and the western half of Russia. Can include normal and alternate (F13.) terrain.

   •      China-Burma-India Theater: Eastern Russia, Mainland Asia/India and Madagascar. Can include PTO (G.), normal, and alternate (F13.) terrain.

   •      Pacific Islands Theater: Australia and all Pacific Islands, including the Aleutians. Can include PTO (G.) and normal terrain.

* SASL rules for Axis Minor, Finnish, and Desert terrain are being developed and will be published at a later date.

12.231 THEATER OPPOSITION CHARTS: Charts are included on the SASL chapter divider to assist players determine which, when, and where, certain nationalities fought each other during World War II. These charts can be helpful, in a general manner, for developing individual Missions (planning the ENEMY, dates, and locations) and for designing an on-going CG (when a theater campaign started and ended for particular nationalities). As an example, on the Western Front, the Germans began with France in September 1939, Norway in April 1940, the Low Countries in May 1940, etc.

12.3 EC/WIND/WEATHER: Randomly determine the Mission's EC (B25.5), Wind Force (B25.63) and weather (E3., F11. or G16).

12.31  WINTER CAMOUFLAGE: In any Mission taking place in any type of snow weather (E3.7) there is a possibility that one or both sides may be Winter Camouflaged (E3.712). Make one dr for each side (applying any applicable drm); on a Final dr of ≤ 2 that side's respective units are considered to have Winter Camouflage for the current Mission. If playing a CG, once the FRIENDLY side has gained Winter Camouflage, it automatically has Winter Camouflage in all other subsequent Missions in which Winter Camouflage would be applicable.

Cumulative drm:

   -2    Finnish

   -1    if the FRIENDLY majority squad type is elite*

   -1    Russian

   +1   1941 German

   +1    for vehicles

   +1    if the FRIENDLY majority squad type is Conscript/Green*

* Applicable to the FRIENDLY side only.

12.32 MISSION TABLES: Tables to determine both sides' SAN and RE #s (2.1), and for determining the starting ENEMY AC# (5.11), are provided in each Mission.

12.321 ENEMY AC# ADJUSTMENT: For Missions occuring as part of a SASL CG, the following adjustments are made to the ENEMY AC#. The ENEMY AC# can never be higher/lower than the levels allowed in the current Mission.

   +1   if the FRIENDLY company majority squad type has an underscored Morale Level of 8.

   -1    if the FRIENDLY company is Italian, Axis Minor, or Chinese.

12.4  ELR: Use the appropriate Chapter H ELR chart to determine the ELR of each side's units (e.g., use H1.28 to determine ELR of German/Russian units). [EXC: the ELR of Axis Minor units is one higher than that listed in H1.28.] If a Night Mission is in effect, modify the Defender ELR per E1.22.

12.5 FRIENDLY WITHDRAWAL: In addition to the method given in the "Mission End" section of each Mission, a Mission also ends immediately upon the exit of the last Mobile/unbroken FRIENDLY unit off the FBE. Each non-Mobile/broken FRIENDLY unit remaining on the map at such a time is considered KIA for (CG) victory condition purposes. Unless a RE instructs otherwise, any Mission ending thusly is an automatic ENEMY victory.

12.6  MISSION VICTORY CONDITIONS: Unless stated otherwise by the Mission, the FRIENDLY side must score more VP than the ENEMY in order to win a SASL Mission. Any other result is an ENEMY win. VP are awarded to each side as per the Mission's VP Schedule. Each Melee existing at Mission end must be carried out until Melee no longer exists. See also 17.1321 if playing a CG.

12.7 MISSION END: SASL Missions do not provide the player with a Turn Record Chart since the turn length of many of them can vary. It is suggested, therefore, that the player use the VP track (of the ASL Scenario Aid Card) as a de facto turn track.

Furthermore, in a Mission where a minimum number of turns that must be played is established at game start, place the "Possible Game End" marker on the number of the first turn that the Mission could possibly end.

12.71 PTO TERRAIN: Due to the congested nature of the terrain when PTO Terrain is in effect for a particular Mission (13.7), the minimum number of turns for a Mission is increased by 1 if PTO terrain is in effect and one or more "w" mapboards are, or become, in play.

12.8 NIGHT: Much of the SASL mechanics of play mirror that of the Night Rules. For example, S? in Hold Attitude (3.1) are similar to not having Freedom of Movement for the Scenario Defender (E1.21), while S? in Advance Attitude (3.2) can represent a Cloaked counter (E1.4). The following rules allow a player to enjoy the thrill of Night combat in the SASL Missions/Campaigns.

12.81 All Night Rules (E1.) are in effect.  Determine NVR (E1.11) normally. If the ENEMY is the Scenario Defender, the ENEMY receives no HIP/Dummy benefits from E1.2.

12.82 CLOAKING: Cloaking is NA for the FRIENDLY player.

12.83 LAX/NORMAL/STEALTHY: Prior to Mission setup, the player determines the Majority Squad Type (MST) of both the FRIENDLY and ENEMY OOB.

12.831 FRIENDLY MST: The status of the FRIENDLY OOB is determined normally based on the Majority Squad Type of the units given in the Mission or in the Campaign Company (use E1.61-.63 as a guide).

12.84 STRAYING: S? in Advance Attitude (3.321) may Stray (E1.53) [EXC: a S? that enters a Location with another S? or activated ENEMY unit is not subject to straying].

12.85 JITTER FIRE: If a S? is the "Closest Defender" or a "Moving unit", roll for Activation (5.). If there is no Activation of the S?, remove it (E1.551).

12.86  S? MOVEMENT: An ENEMY unit will execute a Move Command through non-Illuminated Locations if otherwise given a choice between moving through an Illuminated Location and a non-Illuminated Location.

12.87 ILLUMINATION: Illumination (E1.9) is handled normally for the FRIENDLY side. For the ENEMY, 12.871-.8712 apply in addition to E1.9

12.8711  INITIAL ENEMY USAGE: Until a Starshell has been placed, each ENEMY MMC adds a +1 drm to its Usage dr (E1.921) and each ENEMY unit adds a -1 drm to its Usage dr if there is a FRIENDLY motorized vehicle on board but no ENEMY motorized vehicle on board.

12.8712 ENEMY PLAYER TURN: During determination of ENEMY Actions (6.11), if a Fire Action (Table {A2}) is specified for a unit and there are no FRIENDLY units in LOS, it will make a Starshell usage dr, with any resulting Starshell placed via Method 3. If a FRIENDLY unit is subsequently Illuminated, the Fire Action is executed normally.  Failing the Usage attempt has no effect on the ENEMY unit and it will move per Table {A4}. An ENEMY Leader that is not required to move per 6.317 and is stacked with a unit given a Fire Action will attempt the Starshell Usage dr for that Location.

12.8713  FRIENDLY PLAYER TURN: During determination of ENEMY Actions (6.11), each ENEMY unit will make a Starshell Usage dr at the beginning of the FRIENDLY Movement Phase unless it Panics (6.21).

12.872 IR: If the Prevailing Attitude is Hold, an IR-capable ENEMY FFE:1 is placed as an IR if there is no FRIENDLY unit in its blast area. During the ENEMY Player Turn (only), onboard ENEMY Mortars with IR capability (C8.7 and E1.93) will fire IR if given a Fire Action and no FRIENDLY unit is in LOS.

12.88 ENEMY AFV: Any Activated AFV will become CE if otherwise able to prevent its NVR falling to zero (E1.14).

12.89 RE ADJUSTMENTS: ENEMY RE (Table {A11}) 21, 64-65 are NA at Night. Nationality RE (Table {x9}) 06 and 55 are NA at Night, as is Partisan Table RE 65. Reroll as required.
