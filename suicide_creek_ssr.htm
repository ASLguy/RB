Suicide Creek SSR

SUICIDE CREEK SCENARIO SPECIAL RULES: Unless noted otherwise, all SSR below apply in all CG and non-CG SC scenarios.

SC1 GENERAL

SC1.1 EC, WEATHER, & TERRAIN: EC are Mud, with no wind at start. Weather is Overcast (E3.5) and Mud (E3.6). PTO Terrain (G.1) is in effect [EXC: both Light (G2.1) (EX: D7) and Dense (G2.2) (EX: E8) Jungle exist at the same time – Dense Jungle hexes are painted with a darker shade of green and are also identified by a black center dot inside a white circle]. At night the  Base NVR is 2.

SC1.1 EX: Hex D7 is Light Jungle, indicated by its lighter green color and white center dot. Hex E8 is Dense Jungle, indicated by its dark­er green color and black cen­ter dot inside a white circle.

SC1.2 THE STREAM: For VC/Setup determination purposes (only), “The Stream” consists of the following stream/stream-JD (SSR SC3) hexes (and all stream/stream-JD hexes that connect them): A11-F11-H12- I12-M12-O13-X12.

SC1.21 CREST STATUS: Due to the slippery, high-banked nature of the stream, Crest status (B20.9) is NA in all stream and stream-JD hexes.

SC1.22 VEHICLES: No vehicle may exit a stream hex via a non-stream hexside except from a Ford hex.

SC1.3 SOFT GROUND: Each Bog Check receives the Soft Ground +1 DRM (D8.21) due to Mud.

SC1.4 FOXHOLES: All Infantry in suitable terrain may set up En­trenched (B27.1).

SC1.5 SUICIDE CREEK (SC) PILLBOXES:

SC1.51 LOS: SC Pillboxes must be set up so that their CA allows a unit within them to have a LOS to ≥ 1 stream/allows a unit within them to have a LOS to ≥ 1 stream/stream-JD hex (as if the base level of that hex were Level 0).

SC1.52 SC CELLARS: Each SC Pillbox has a separate Location that is treated as a 1+4+∞ Pillbox Location ex­cept as stated otherwise. A SC Cellar is Accessible from, and has LOS to, only its Pillbox, and movement between the two Locations is not prevented by enemy units in the same hex outside. IFT-fire/DC-placement/CC is allowed only between the SC Cellar and its Pillbox, with the Pillbox Location receiving a +3 TEM. Control of a Pillbox hex (B30.91) also requires Control of the SC Cellar. The SC Cellar and all units in it are eliminated if its Pillbox is eliminated (B30.92).

SC1.53 BUNKERS & TUNNELS: Bunkers (B30.8) and tunnels (G1.632) are NA.

SC1.54 BAZOOKAS: The colored dr of a Bazooka TH DR which targets a SC Pillbox must also be ≤ 4 to achieve a hit. If the colored dr is ≥ 5, the bazooka round is assumed to have glanced off its slick mud covering and is considered a miss.

SC1.55 HIP: Once revealed, SC Pillboxes may never regain hidden status.

SC1.6 OBSERVERS: Any leader possessing a radio at scenario start may set up using HIP.

SC1.7 HAND-TO-HAND CC: Hand-to-Hand CC (J2.31) may also be declared by an American ATTACKER after a successful Ambush unless every American unit participating is Withdrawing/Pinned.

SC1.8 ASSAULT ENGINEERS: American 7-6-8 squads and their 3-4-8 HS are Assault Engineers (H1.22) [EXC: 7-6- 8 squads may not Deploy per G17.11]. In the Campaign Game, the Japanese may purchase Assault Engineers. Special counters with a DC symbol have been provided for both cases to allow players to distinguish them from non-Assault Engineer MMC.

SC1.9 ATMM: In addition to G1.4231, Japanese units that are not T-H Heroes may use ATMM as per C13.7 [EXC: the CC DRM for a non-T-H Hero is only -2].

SC2 JUNGLE DEBRIS

 

SC2.1 Jungle Debris (JD) represents fallen, exceptionally tangled jungle. Any hex containing mixed brown and green rubble-like artwork is a JD hex (EX: K12). JD is treated as Dense Jungle except as stated otherwise [EXC: JD is not treated as Dense Jungle for purposes of NVR/Illumina­tion/MTR -fire (G2.24)].

SC2.2 Pillboxes and Wire are the only Fortifications that may be set up in JD.

SC2.3 JD is Inherent Terrain (B.6) and has a TEM of +1 to any fire traced into it. Air Bursts (B13.3) are NA in a JD hex. A JD hex is not a LOS obstacle but it does Hinder LOS by adding a +2 Hindrance DRM for each JD hex between the firer and target.

SC2.4 MOVEMENT: Infantry enter JD at a cost of 3 MF [EXC: if using a path/TB/CR (SSR SC4)]. Only a fully-tracked vehicle may enter JD, and does so by ex­pending all of its MP (other than for Start, Stop, or if using Minimum Move) allotment and making a Bog Check DR (D8.21). Such movement is possible even while using reverse movement. A fully-tracked vehicle may also enter a JD hex (other than via TB/CR) by expending ½  of its MP allotment (FRU) in that hex but must make a Bog Check DR with an extra +1 DRM.

SC3 STREAM-JUNGLE DEBRIS

SC3.1 Stream-Jungle Debris (stream-JD) is treated as JD (SSR SC2) ex­cept as stated otherwise [EXC: for purposes of E1 .531, it is treated as a stream].

SC3.2 JD exists at crest level in a stream-JD hex, but units in a stream- JD hex are always at stream level (SC1.21); however, the JD is consid­ered to extend down to stream level for purposes of both TEM and LOS Hindrance; i.e., a unit in a stream-JD hex always receives the +1 TEM of JD, and LOS traced through a stream-JD hex is subject to a +2 Hin­drance DRM for each stream-JD hex between the firer and the target.

SC3.2 Stream-JD LOS EX: Fire from the Pillbox in V12 against units in the S11 stream receives a +2 Hindrance DRM because the LOS passes through the stream-JD hex in T11, even though the firer and the target are at different levels.

SC3.3 MOVEMENT: Infantry enter stream-JD at a cost of 5 MF. Only a fully-tracked vehicle may enter stream-JD at a cost of all MP [EXC: Stop/Start MP] and must make a Bog Check DR with an extra +3 DRM.

SC4 CORDUROY ROADS

SC4.1 Corduroy Roads (CR) were used to make swampy or muddy terrain traversable by laying logs in a pattern that re­sembled corduroy. CR are treated as Trail Breaks (B13.421) except as stated otherwise.

SC4.2 CREATION: CR can only be created prior to play by SSR, or by CG purchase. CR may only be placed in Jungle/JD hexes (not in stream-JD hexes). In addition to providing CR counters, a SSR may treat paths as CR. If so, the CR hexside of any CR counter is treated as con­necting to the CR in the ex-path hex.

SC4.3 INFANTRY: Infantry expend one MF to enter Jungle/JD hex via a CR, and are not subject to the B13.4212 special Defensive First Fire -1 DRM.

SC4.4 VEHICLES: Fully-tracked vehicles expend two MP to enter Jun­gle/JD hex via a CR; halftracks expend three MP. A hex entered via a CR hexside does not count toward the number of hexes moved into for the purpose of D8.23. A wreck/immobile vehicle on a CR does not elimi­nate/negate it.

SC5 FORDS & FORD CREATION

SC5.1 Fords may be placed (at setup by SSR) or created (during play) in stream hexes (but not in stream-JD hexes).

SC5.2 CREATION: To create a Ford during play, a bull­dozer must expend all of its MP [EXC: Start MP] allotment in the hex where Ford creation will be attempted and end its MPh in Motion. Provided the bulldozer remains Mobile after all Defensive First Fire directed against it, the American player may make a DR.  If this DR is ≤ 5 a Ford is created at the end of the MPh. If the creation attempt fails a Labor (B24.8) counter is placed (or an existing counter is flipped to its -2 DRM side), and may be used to modify sub­sequent Ford creation attempts in the same hex.

SC5.3 HEROES: Each participating hero may apply its heroic DRM to a Ford creation attempt by declaring the at­tempt, expending all of its MF allotment as Hazardous Movement in Armored Assault with the bulldozer, surviving the MPh un­pinned, and being marked with a TI counter.

SC6 TRIP WIRES

SC6.1 Trip Wires were improvised devices created by the Americans to detect Japanese infiltration into their lines during night operations. Trip Wires consisted of strings and wires set at varying heights and draped with objects which made noise if disturbed. Trip Wires are treated as Trip Flares (E1.95) except as stated otherwise.

SC6.2 EFFECTS: A Trip Wire that has been set off causes the moving/advancing/withdrawing unit(s) (only) to immediately lose any concealed/cloaked status, provided there are unbroken enemy Infantry unit(s) adja­cent to that Trip Wire location. Trip Wires do not provide Illumination, though they may be set up in the same Location as Trip Flares. Separate drs are required for any Location that has both Trip Wires and Trip Flares. A Trip Wire that has been set off is equivalent to a fired Starshell for the purpose of allowing Fire Lanes vs Bore Sighted hexes (E1.71) and the subsequent usage of Starshells/IR (E1.9).

SC7 SUPPLY DUMPS

SC7.1 In some SC scenarios the Americans are allotted Sup­ply Dumps – ammunition and stores which were brought for­ward to aid the Marines in their assault across Suicide Creek. The Japanese made several attempts to destroy these dumps. Use Ammo Supply counters to represent these Supply Dumps.

SC7.2 SETUP: A Supply Dump may set up using HIP and must set up in/adjacent to ≥ 1 kunai hex.

SC7.3 DISCOVERY: The Japanese player detects the presence of a Supply Dump by either entering its location or by successfully Searching its location. The Supply Dump counter is then placed on board.

SC7.4 DESTRUCTION: A Supply Dump may be eliminated in three ways:

•DC: A DC may be detonated in the Supply Dump’s location; any result other than a Malfunction will eliminate the Supply Dump. A Supply Dump is an eligible DC Hero target (G1.424).

•IFT attack: When the Supply Dump’s Location (including the Supply Dump itself, if Known, and/or an enemy unit therein) is attacked on the IFT by ≥ 8 FP the Supply Dump is eliminated on a subsequent dr of 1. This subsequent dr is modified by -1 for each additional column on the IFT that is applied to the attack, i.e., an attack on the 8 FP will destroy the Supply Dump on a subsequent dr of 1; a 12 FP attack will destroy it on a subsequent dr of ≤ 2, etc.

•Terrain Blaze: A Supply Dump in a terrain Blaze is eliminated.

SC7.5 EXPLOSION: If the Supply Dump is eliminated it immediately detonates; all units in the Location are attacked with 16 FP and no DRM. After detonating, replace the Ammo Supply counter with a Flame counter and place a dis­persed Smoke counter in its hex. This Smoke counter is removed in the following PFPh, regardless of Player Turn.
